    Early Life & Biography

    The child singer was born in Atlanta, Georgia, on 31 July 2007. Her parents are James Hale and Eva Bolando. Angelica was born in a family of two children and had a younger sister by the name of Abigail. The father of Angelica is half white and half Filipino, while her mother is from a city in the Philippines called Iligan City.

    When Angelica was four years old, she contracted severe bacterial pneumonia, which caused her septic shock and multiple organ failure. Her kidney failed, and she suffered permanent scarring on her lung. She was placed in a medically induced coma and was placed on ECMO life-support, where she stayed for 12 days. After being discharged from the hospital, she stayed for one year on dialysis, and later she received a kidney transplant from her mother, Eva.

    After her recovery, she began taking singing lessons, and she won a singing competition for five to nine-year-olds. Since then, she has performed in several charitable functions like Children’s Healthcare of Atlanta and Children’s Miracle Network Hospitals.

    Personal Life

    Angelica was named as the national spokesperson for “It’s About Time.” This is a Sepsis Alliance campaign that raises awareness of sepsis as a medical emergency. For her former condition, she continues to have quarterly check-ups with her doctors to assess her healing process as well as her progress.

    Career

    Hale competed on season 12 of America’s Got Talent show, and that is when her fame started. During the auditions, she performed the song by Andra Day titled “Rise Up.” During the Judge Cuts round, she did a cover of Alicia Keys’ song “Girl on Fire,” and she was given a golden buzzer by Chris Hardwick, who was the guest judge.

    She sang the song “Clarity” by Zedd and Foxes during the live shows at the Quarterfinals, and during the Semi-finals, she did the song “Without You” by David Guetta. She advanced to the Top 10 finals after she performed a song by Clean Bandit and Zara Larsson called “Symphony.”

    During the final episode of the show, Angelica performed the song titled “Stronger (What Doesn’t Kill You)” by Kelly Clarkson. She did the song together with Kelly and a fellow contestant named Kechi Okwuchi. She finished the show in the second position, becoming the youngest runner-up in the history of the show.

    After America’s Got Talent show, Angelica continued performing on different occasions. She performed in front of a crowd of 7,000 people in Tuckahoe, New York. From November 2-5, she performed at the AGT live shows at Los Vegas alongside Preacher Lawson, Light Balance, and Darci Lynne.

    In 2017 she performed at an NFL game between the Dallas Cowboys and Atlanta Falcons at the Mercedes-Benz Stadium in Atlanta. She performed the National Anthem during the Baltimore Ravens and Pittsburgh Steelers game. She performed the song “Santa Claus is Coming to Town” during the half-time of the NFL game between Arizona Cardinals and Atlanta Falcons. The song was from her Christmas album called “Christmas Wonder.”

    Angelica released her debut single titled “Feel the Magic on Google Play, Apple Music Spotify, and Amazon Music. She was the writer of the single and the music video premiered on YouTube and Facebook in May 2019. She joined the Filipino boy band TNT Boys on their world tour in April 2019.

    As an actress, Angelica appeared on the series Maya Unstoppable which premiered in2019 as well as the movie “American Reject” in 2019.
