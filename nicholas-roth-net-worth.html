    FAQs about Nicholas Roth

    Who is Nicholas Roth?

    He is an American classical pianist, recording artist, and professor of piano at Drake University in Des Moines, Iowa. He is known for his performances both as a soloist and in collaboration with orchestras and other musicians.

    What is his educational background?

    Roth holds a Doctor of Musical Arts degree from Michigan State University, Artist Diplomas from the Hochschule für Musik in Munich, and both MM and BM degrees from Indiana University. He has received numerous accolades and scholarships throughout his educational journey.

    With which orchestras has Nicholas Roth performed?

    He has performed with the St. Louis Symphony and the Indianapolis Symphony Orchestra, among others, often under the direction of notable conductors like Raymond Leppard.

    What recordings has Roth released?

    Roth has recorded works by Schumann, Scriabin, Rachmaninov, Blumenfeld, and Grieg. His recordings are available on the Blue Griffin, Naxos, MSR Classics, and Innova labels.

    Has Roth received any awards or recognitions?

    Yes, he was a 1993 Beethoven Fellow of the American Pianists Association and has received critical acclaim for his musical performances. He has also been honored by the Indiana House of Representatives for his contributions to the arts.

    Where has Nicholas Roth taught?

    Roth has taught masterclasses worldwide, including at Indiana University, Vanderbilt University, and the University of Chicago. He is also involved in educational activities in various international festivals and music schools.

    Does Roth participate in any music festivals?

    Yes, Roth is actively involved in various international music festivals, such as the Ono Zone International Music Festival in Quito, Ecuador, and the Classical Music Festival Piano Seminar in Eisenstadt, Austria.
